Here’s the **full description of the Wix Orders CSV export format** for **item-level reporting** when doing a complete data dump:

***

## ✅ **File Format**

*   **Type:** CSV (Comma-Separated Values)
*   **Encoding:** UTF-8
*   **Delimiter:** Comma
*   **Time Zone:** All timestamps in **UTC**
*   **Structure:** Each purchased item appears as a separate row, even if multiple items belong to the same order.
*   **Header Row:** Column names provided by Wix; do **not** rename or remove columns when re-importing.

***

## ✅ **Typical Columns in Item-Level Export**

*(Exact names may vary slightly by Wix version, but these are standard fields)*

### **Order-Level Fields**

*   `Order ID` – Unique Wix order identifier
*   `Order Number` – Sequential number for display
*   `Order Date` – UTC timestamp of purchase
*   `Order Status` – e.g., Paid, Fulfilled, Cancelled
*   `Payment Status` – Paid / Pending / Refunded
*   `Payment Method` – e.g., Credit Card, PayPal
*   `Currency` – ISO code (e.g., USD)
*   `Subtotal` – Before tax/shipping
*   `Tax` – Tax amount
*   `Shipping` – Shipping cost
*   `Discount` – Discount applied
*   `Total` – Grand total
*   `Channel` – Sales channel (Website, POS, etc.)

### **Item-Level Fields**

*   `Product Name` – Name of the purchased product
*   `SKU` – Product SKU (if defined)
*   `Quantity` – Number of units purchased
*   `Price` – Unit price at time of sale
*   `Line Total` – Price × Quantity
*   `Variant Options` – e.g., Size: 24x36; Media: Metal
*   `Product ID` – Internal Wix product identifier

### **Customer Fields**

*   `Customer Name`
*   `Email`
*   `Phone`
*   `Billing Address` – Full formatted address
*   `Shipping Address` – Full formatted address

### **Additional Fields**

*   `Notes` – Seller notes or buyer comments
*   `Tracking Number` – If fulfillment completed
*   `Fulfillment Status` – e.g., Shipped, Delivered
*   `Refund Status` – If applicable

***

## ✅ **Behavior**

*   You can **deselect columns** before export, but for a full dump, keep all columns.
*   Each row = **one item**; multi-item orders will have repeated order-level data across rows.
*   Export includes **all selected orders**; max rows depend on number of items.
